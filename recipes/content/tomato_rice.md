# Tomato Rice

### Prep Steps

<!-- List prep tasks here -->

### Ingredients
- 1 tablespoon oil (preferably sesame or any cooking oil)
- 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
- 1 teaspoon chana dal
- 1 teaspoon urad dal
- 1 dried red chili, broken into pieces
- A few curry leaves
- 1smallonion, finely chopped
- 1mediumtomato, chopped
- 1 tablespoon tamarind concentrate (or soak a lemon-sized tamarind in 1 cup warm water and extract juice)
- 2cupswater
- 1½teaspoons sambar powder (or 1 teaspoon coriander powder + ½ teaspoon chili powder)
- ¼teaspoon turmeric powder
- Salt to taste
- ½teaspoon jaggery or sugar (optional, to balance tanginess)
- 1 teaspoon rice flour (optional, for thickening)
- 1cuprice (preferably sona masuri), soaked for 20 minutes
- 2 tablespoons grated coconut (optional)
- 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves
- 2 tablespoons chopped mint leaves (optional)​

### Instructions
1. Prep:Chop 1 small onion and 1 medium tomato.
2. If using tamarind instead of concentrate, soak and extract juice.
3. Soak 1 cup rice for 20 minutes, then drain.
4. Mix 1 teaspoon rice flour in 2 tablespoons water and keep aside (if using).
5. Cook:Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a pressure cooker. Add 1 teaspoon mustard seeds and let them splutter.
6. Add 1 teaspoon chana dal, 1 teaspoon urad dal, 1 dried red chili (broken), and a few curry leaves. Fry until the dals turn golden.
7. Add the chopped onion and sauté until soft and slightly golden.
8. Add the chopped tomato and cook until it turns soft and mushy.
9. Add 1½ teaspoons sambar powder, ¼ teaspoon turmeric powder, and salt to taste. Stir well.
10. Add the tamarind concentrate mixed with 2 cups water (or your tamarind extract). Let it boil.
11. Add the soaked and drained rice. Mix well.
12. Add 2 tablespoons grated coconut, 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves, and 2 tablespoons chopped mint leaves (if using).
13. Close the pressure cooker lid and cook for 2 whistles on medium heat. Then turn off the heat and let the pressure release naturally.
14. Once the pressure has released, open the lid and add ½ teaspoon jaggery or sugar to balance the tanginess. Mix gently.
