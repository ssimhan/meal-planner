Protein Banana Muffins

To Edit, To Try Out

Prep Time: 10 minutes Cook Time: 14 minutes Servings: 12 Source: yummytoddlerfood.com

Ingredients

1 cup Greek yogurt

1 cup mashed banana (2 large)

½ cup creamy peanut butter

⅓ cup maple syrup

3 eggs (lightly beaten)

2 teaspoons vanilla extract

1½ cups all-purpose flour

1 teaspoon baking powder

½ teaspoon baking soda

1 teaspoon cinnamon

¼ teaspoon salt

⅓ cup protein powder (optional)

mini chocolate chips (optional)

Directions

Prep:

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

Grease a standard 12-cup muffin tin with nonstick spray or line with paper liners.


Cook:

In a large bowl, combine the Greek yogurt, mashed bananas, peanut butter, maple syrup, eggs, and vanilla extract. Mix until smooth.

Add the flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt to the wet ingredients. If using protein powder or other optional add-ins like chocolate chips or blueberries, fold them in now. Stir gently until just combined; avoid overmixing.

Divide the batter evenly among the prepared muffin cups, filling each about three-quarters full.

Bake for 14-16 minutes, or until the edges are lightly golden and a toothpick inserted into the center of a muffin comes out clean.

Allow the muffins to cool in the pan for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.


Enjoy your muffins!

Notes

Store in an airtight container
3 days for counter, 5 days for fridge

You can also freeze the muffins in a freezer bag with as much air removed as possible for up to 3 months. Thaw at room temperature or in 15-second increments in the microwave.

Peanut-free: Use melted butter for the peanut butter.

You can use a regular 9×5-inch loaf pan or a mini loaf pan. They work the same; the only difference is the baking time.
