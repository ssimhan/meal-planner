Rasam Rice with Carrot Kai

South Indian Fusion Soup-Rice Comfort Food
Prep Time: 15 min Cook Time: 25 min Servings: 2

Ingredients

For Rasam:

1 tsp tamarind concentrate

1 medium tomato, chopped

1 tsp black pepper (coarsely crushed)

1 tsp cumin seeds

2 garlic cloves, crushed

1/4 tsp turmeric powder

1/2 tsp rasam powder (or use more black pepper + cumin if unavailable)

Salt to taste

3 cups water

For Tempering:

1 tsp mustard seeds

1/2 tsp cumin seeds

1 dry red chilli (optional)

A pinch of asafoetida (hing)

1 sprig curry leaves

1 tsp ghee or oil

For Rice and Carrot Kai:

1 cup cooked rice

2 medium carrots, peeled and diced small

1/4 tsp turmeric powder

1/2 tsp red chilli powder (adjust to taste)

Salt to taste

1 tsp oil

Directions

Chop 1 medium tomato.

Crush 2 garlic cloves.

Peel and dice 2 medium carrots into small cubes.

Cook 1 cup rice and keep it ready (preferably slightly mushy, like for rasam rice).

In a pot, mix 1 tsp tamarind concentrate with 3 cups water.

Add chopped tomato, crushed garlic, 1/4 tsp turmeric powder, 1 tsp black pepper, 1 tsp cumin seeds, 1/2 tsp rasam powder, and salt.

Let it simmer on medium heat for 10–12 minutes until tomatoes soften and flavors blend. Don't boil too hard—rasam should be light.

In a small pan, heat 1 tsp ghee or oil.

Add 1 tsp mustard seeds. When they splutter, add 1/2 tsp cumin seeds, 1 red chilli, pinch of hing, and curry leaves.

Pour this tempering into the simmering rasam. Turn off heat.

Heat 1 tsp oil in a pan.

Add diced carrots and sauté on medium heat for 4–5 minutes.

Sprinkle 1/4 tsp turmeric powder, 1/2 tsp red chilli powder, and salt.

Stir and cook until carrots are tender (about 5–6 more minutes).

In a bowl or plate, mix cooked rice with hot rasam.

Top or serve it with the sautéed carrot kai on the side or mixed in.
