Sheet Pan Black Bean Quesadillas with Coleslaw

Mexican, To Edit, To Try Out

Prep Time: 10 minutes Cook Time: 10 minutes Servings: 6 Source: yummytoddlerfood.com

Ingredients

Flour tortillas

2 cans black beans

Shredded cheese, cheddar

Avocado

Cumin powder

Salsa, optional

Sour cream, optional

Guacamole, optional

Coleslaw bag

Mayo

Lime juice

Garlic powder

Red chili powder

Directions

Preheat oven to 375 degrees F and line 2 baking sheets with parchment paper.

Place the tortillas on a baking sheet. They can overlap a little. Spread 1 mashed avocado on half of each tortilla. (You'll leave one half empty so you can fold it over to make a half-moon shape.)

Top puree with ¼ cup beans, ¼ cheese, and a sprinkle of cumin.

Bake for 5- 7 minutes or until heated through and melty. Fold the empty side of the tortilla over and press down. Flip over and bake for an additional 2-3 minutes and remove from the oven.

While in the oven, take the coleslaw mix, rinse with water and drain well.

Add some mayo, lime juice, salt, red chili powder and garlic powder.

Serve quesadillas with side of coleslaw.


Alternative to coleslaw:
Cucumber, tomatoes, oil, vinegar or lime juice, salt and pepper to taste with some cilantro

Notes

Store any leftovers in an airtight container or wrapped in foil for 3-5 days. Warm in the oven or microwave to heat through.

Use corn or flour tortillas as you like. (If using corn, look for ones labeled "soft" to help ensure they don't crack or break.)

Serve with diced fruit, corn, or other simple side.
