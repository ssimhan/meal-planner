Vegetable Lasagna

Dinner, Italian, To Try Out

Prep Time: 45 min Cook Time: 45 minutes Total Time: 120 Servings: 8 Source: theseasonedmom.com

Ingredients

2 teaspoons olive oil

1 zucchini

1 bell pepper, red

1 onion

1 tablespoon garlic

1 cup spinach

1 egg, beaten

2 tablespoons parsley

2 tbsp basil

15 ounces ricotta cheese

2 ¼ cups grated mozzarella cheese

½ cup grated Parmesan cheese

24 ounces marinara sauce

No-boil lasagna noodles

Directions

Preheat oven to 400 degrees F. Spray a 9 x 13-inch baking dish with cooking spray and set aside.

Dice zucchini, bell pepper, onion and spinach.

In a large skillet or Dutch oven, heat oil over medium-high heat. Sauté veggies and garlic.

Use a separate bowl to stir together egg, parsley, basil, ricotta cheese, 1 ¼ cups mozzarella, and ¼ cup Parmesan.

When the vegetables are done cooking, stir the marinara sauce into the skillet.

Spread ¼ of the marinara-vegetable sauce mixture in a thin layer in the bottom of the prepared baking dish.

Create layer of no-boil lasagna noodles, ⅓ of the ricotta mixture, and another ¼ of the sauce mixture.

Repeat layers two more times. Finish with remaining 1 cup of mozzarella and ¼ cup of Parmesan cheese on top. Cover with aluminum foil.

Bake for 35 minutes.

Remove foil, and bake for another 5-10 minutes (until cheese on top is browned).

Let stand for about 10 minutes before slicing and serving.

Notes

Make Ahead

Assemble the lasagna in advance, but do not bake. Wrap tightly and store in the refrigerator for up to 48 hours before baking, or freeze for up to 3 months.

When ready to bake, allow the refrigerated lasagna to sit on the counter and come to room temperature for at least 30-60 minutes before baking.

Leftover lasagna will keep in an airtight container in the refrigerator for 3-4 days. You can also freeze the leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 months.
