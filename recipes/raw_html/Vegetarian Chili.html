Vegetarian Chili

Dinner, Instant Pot, Soup, To Try Out

Prep Time: 15 mins Cook Time: 35 mins Servings: Servings: 6 servings Source: wellplated.com

Ingredients

1 tablespoon olive oil

1 onion

2 sweet potatoes

2 bell peppers, red

4 cloves garlic

Chili powder

Chipotle chili powder

Cumin powder

Salt

2 1/2 cups vegetable broth

1 can tomato sauce, 8oz

1/2 cup uncooked quinoa

1 can black beans

1 can kidney beans

1/2 teaspoon granulated sugar

Cilantro

Shredded cheese

Crushed tortilla chips

Greek yogurt

Directions

Dice yellow onion, sweet potatoes, red bell peppers.


Once the oil is hot, add the onion and cook until beginning to soften, about 3 minutes.

Add the sweet potatoes, bell pepper, garlic, chili powder, chipotle chili powder, cumin, and kosher salt.

Cook for 1 to 2 minutes, until the garlic is fragrant.

Add 2.5 cups of the vegetable broth and the quinoa.

Pour the tomato sauce on top (do not stir again).

Cover and seal the Instant Pot. Cook on manual (HIGH) pressure for 8 minutes. Immediately release the pressure. Carefully open the Instant Pot.

Turn the Instant Pot to OFF. Stir in the black beans, kidney beans, and sugar. Place the lid back on top and let stand 10 minutes to thicken. Uncover. Taste and adjust seasoning as desired. Serve hot, with any and all the toppings!

Vegetarian chili is good in the refrigerator for 5 days and in the freezer for 3 months. Let the leftovers cool completely, and store them in an airtight container.


Can serve with salad, cornbread or roasted veggies
